Transportation

Getting to Academia Sinica

By Airport MRT & Metro (Recommended)

  1. Airport MRT (Express): Airport → Taipei Main Station (39 mins, NT$160).
  2. MRT Blue Line: Transfer to Blue Line heading to Nangang Exhibition Center (terminal station, 21 mins, NT$30).
  3. From "Nangang Exhibition Center" bus stop:
    • Taxi: 5–10 mins to Academia Sinica (approx. NT$100–120).
    • Bus: Take 212, 306, 205, 620, or 645 to "Academia Sinica" bus stop.

By Direct Airport Bus

  • Take Kuo-Kuang Bus 1843 to Nangang Exhibition Center (80–90 mins, NT$158).
  • Note: please check the current timetable on the official website before boarding.
  • Transfer to a short taxi ride or local bus (as listed above) to the venue.

By Taxi / Uber (24/7)

  • Boarding: look for official taxi queues outside Arrival Lobbies (T1: Gate 12 / T2: Gate 26).
  • Time & fare: Approx. 45–50 mins (subject to traffic), costing around NT$1,500–1,700.
  • For driver: Show this Chinese address - 中央研究院（台北市南港區研究院路二段128號）

By Bus (Most Cost-Effective)

  • Go to the "MRT Nangang Station" bus stop.
  • Take bus 212 (Direct/直), 270, or Blue 25 (藍25).
  • Get off at the "Academia Sinica" bus stop (approx. 15–20 mins, NT$15).

By Taxi / Uber (Fastest)

  • Boarding: follow the station signs to the designated taxi pickup areas on the 1st floor (Level 1) or outside the main exit.
  • Travel time & fare: Approx. 10–15 minutes (subject to traffic), costing around NT$120–150.
  • For driver: Show this Chinese address - 中央研究院（台北市南港區研究院路二段128號）

By Metro & Walk / Bus

  • You can also take the MRT Blue Line from Nangang Station to Nangang Exhibition Center Station (1 stop, 2 mins, NT$20).
  • From Exit 5, it is a 5-minute taxi ride (as listed above) or a 20-minute walk to the venue.

By Bus (Most Cost-Effective)

  1. Go to "Nangang Exhibition Center" bus stop.
  2. Take bus 212, 306, 205, 620, or 645 to "Academia Sinica" bus stop.

Explore Taipei

Places to visit in Taipei

Taipei Skyline

A city view blending Taipei 101 with the surrounding mountains, capturing the scale of the capital.

CKS Memorial Hall

A landmark monument and plaza in central Taipei, popular for its architecture and changing-of-the-guard ceremony.

National Theater

A classical Chinese-style performance venue facing the National Concert Hall across Liberty Square.

Taipei Dome

A modern multi-purpose arena hosting sports events and concerts in the Songshan district.

Taipei Main Station

The city's central transport hub, connecting rail, metro, and high-speed rail lines.

Grand Hotel Taipei

A landmark red-and-gold hotel on Yuanshan overlooking the Keelung River and city skyline.

Jiufen Old Street

A hillside street lined with tea houses and lantern-lit alleys, known for its views over the coast.

Jiufen Mountain Village

Terraced streets climbing the hillside, offering panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and sea.

Tamsui

A riverside district known for sunset views, an old street, and waterfront cafés.

Beitou

A hot spring district with thermal valley scenery and mountain views.

Raohe Street Night Market

One of Taipei’s oldest night markets, known for local street food and a lively evening atmosphere.

Songshan Ciyou Temple

A historic temple at the entrance to Raohe Street Night Market, dedicated to the sea goddess Mazu.
